What is Trojan Malware?

A Trojan, or Trojan horse, is a type of malware that disguises itself as legitimate software to trick users into downloading and executing it. Unlike viruses, Trojans do not replicate themselves but can create backdoors, steal data, or install additional malware once inside a system (Symantec, 2021).

Trojans often spread through malicious email attachments, fake software downloads, or compromised websites. Because they appear harmless, many users unknowingly install them, allowing attackers to gain control over their systems.

What Does a Trojan Do to a Computer?

Once executed, a Trojan can have severe consequences, including:

  • Data Theft – Many Trojans log keystrokes, steal passwords, or extract sensitive financial information.
  • System Hijacking – Some Trojans create backdoors, allowing hackers to remotely control the infected device.
  • Download and Install More Malware – Trojans can serve as a gateway for additional threats like ransomware, spyware, or cryptocurrency miners.
  • Disrupt System Performance – Many Trojans consume CPU and memory resources, slowing down computers or causing crashes.
  • Turn Devices into Botnets – Some Trojans convert infected computers into bots for launching cyberattacks, such as Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks (McAfee, 2022).

How to Combat and Remove Trojans

Preventing Trojan Infections

  • Download Software from Official Sources – Avoid third-party websites or torrents that may distribute infected files.
  • Use Strong Security Software – Antivirus programs like Norton, Malwarebytes, and Windows Defender can detect and block Trojans.
  • Be Wary of Email Attachments & Links – Phishing emails often contain Trojans disguised as invoices, receipts, or job offers.
  • Keep Your Software Updated – Security patches help fix vulnerabilities that Trojans exploit.

Question: What's one of the best ways to avoid downloading a Trojan?

Removing a Trojan from a Computer

  • Disconnect from the Internet – This prevents the Trojan from communicating with its remote server.
  • Boot into Safe Mode – Running the system in Safe Mode can stop the Trojan from executing.
  • Run a Full Antivirus Scan – Use reputable security software to detect and quarantine malicious files.
  • Check Installed Programs & Processes – Uninstall suspicious applications and terminate unknown background processes.
  • Restore or Reinstall the Operating System – If the infection is severe, a system restore or clean OS installation may be necessary.

Conclusion

Trojans are one of the most deceptive and dangerous forms of malware, often leading to data breaches, financial losses, or complete system compromise. By practicing safe online habits, using security tools, and staying vigilant against suspicious software, users can protect their devices from Trojan infections.
