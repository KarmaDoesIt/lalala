What is a Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software (malware) that attaches itself to a program or file and spreads when executed. Like a biological virus, it can replicate itself and infect other files or systems, often without the user's knowledge. The term "virus" was first coined in the 1980s, though the concept of self-replicating programs dates back to earlier decades (Cohen, 1987).

What Does a Computer Virus Do?

Computer viruses come in many forms and serve different malicious purposes. Some of the common effects of a virus include:

  • Data Corruption - Viruses may delete or alter files, making them unusable.
  • System Slowdown - Many viruses consume system resources, causing computers to lag.
  • Unauthorized Access - Some viruses allow hackers to gain control of a system.
  • Spreading to Other Devices - Certain viruses replicate through USB drives, networks, or email attachments.
  • Ransomware Attacks - Some modern viruses, like ransomware, lock users out of their files until a ransom is paid (Europol, 2021).

How to Combat and Remove Computer Viruses

Prevention is Key!

  • Use Antivirus Software - Programs like Windows Defender, Norton, or McAfee help detect and remove viruses.
  • Keep Software Updated - Security patches help fix vulnerabilities that viruses exploit.
  • Avoid Suspicious Links and Attachments - Phishing emails and unverified downloads are common infection methods.
  • Enable a Firewall - Firewalls act as a barrier to prevent unauthorized access.

How to Remove a Virus

  • 1. Enter Safe Mode - Booting into Safe Mode prevents many virsues from running.
  • 2. Run a Full Antivirus Scan - Use an updated antivirus program to detect and quarantine threats.
  • 2. Delete Temporary Files - This removes some hidden malicious scripts.
  • 4. Restore the System - If the virus is too deep-rooted, a system restore or factory reset may be necessary.

Conclusion

Computer viruses remain a significant cybersecurity threat, but understanding how they work and how to prevent infections can protect individuals and organizations. By using security software, practicing safe browsing habits, and keeping systems updated, users can reduce their risk of falling victimm to malicious programs.
