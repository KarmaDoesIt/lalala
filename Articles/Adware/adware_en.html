Adware Malware Article 

What is Adware Malware? 

Adware (short for "advertising-supported software") is a type of malware designed to automatically display, download, or inject advertisements into a user's system. While some adware is relatively harmless and legally bundled with free software, malicious adware can track user activity, slow down systems, and expose users to further threats (Symantec, 2021).

Adware often infiltrates computers through freeware downloads, software bundling, and deceptive pop-ups that trick users into clicking malicious links. Unlike legitimate advertising software, harmful adware operates without consent, displaying intrusive ads and collecting personal data for financial gain.

What Does Adware Do to a Computer? 

Once installed, adware can cause several issues, including:

  1. Intrusive Pop-Up Ads – Unwanted ads appear frequently, disrupting browsing and reducing productivity.
  1. Browser Hijacking – Some adware changes browser settings, redirecting users to shady websites.
  1. System Slowdowns – Excessive ads and background processes consume memory and CPU power.
  1. Data Collection & Privacy Risks – Some adware tracks browsing habits and sells user data to advertisers or cybercriminals (McAfee, 2022).
  1. Increased Security Risks – Clicking on adware-generated pop-ups may lead to additional malware infections, such as spyware or ransomware.

How to Combat and Remove Adware 

Preventing Adware Infections 

  1. Download Software from Trusted Sources – Avoid third-party sites that bundle adware with downloads.
  1. Use a Reputable Antivirus & Anti-Adware Tool – Programs like Malwarebytes, AdwCleaner, or Windows Defender help detect and block adware.
  1. Be Cautious with Free Software – Always choose “Custom Installation” to deselect unwanted add-ons.
  1. Enable a Pop-Up Blocker – Modern browsers offer built-in tools to prevent malicious pop-ups.

Removing Adware from a Computer 

  1. Uninstall Suspicious Programs – Check the installed applications list and remove unfamiliar or unwanted software.
  1. Reset Web Browsers – Clear browser settings, cache, and extensions that may be linked to adware.
  1. Run an Anti-Adware Scan – Use security tools to detect and remove adware infections.
  1. Check the Hosts File & DNS Settings – Malicious adware may modify these settings to control network traffic.
  1. Perform a System Restore – If adware persists, restoring the system to an earlier clean state may be necessary.

Conclusion 

Adware is a persistent and often underestimated cybersecurity threat that disrupts user experience and compromises privacy. By practicing safe browsing habits, using security tools, and carefully managing software installations, users can effectively prevent and remove adware from their systems.
